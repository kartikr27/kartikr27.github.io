The Process

My friends from Dallas, Austin, and Houston collaborated on a robot to compete at the annual offseason competition Texas Robotics Invitational in Houston. Naming our team "Washed Up" having a surftide theme. We decided to develop a simple robot based off of a successful archetype used during the season and quickly design, manufacture, and code this robot in under 3 days. We utilized workspaces and resoruces from our teams which allowed us to complete our project. Behind the Bumpers, a famous YouTube channel that creates FRC content, interviewed us on our design process for this robot.

I developed most of the robot's code controlling the drivebase, arm and wrist joints, intake rollers, and a simple autonomous routine. I utilized trapezoidal motion profiling to allow smooth joint movements in concurrence with accurate setpoints to efficiently optimize the subsystems. I was thrilled to be leading the software side of this project and enjoy this time with my peers before we all head different ways toward college.
